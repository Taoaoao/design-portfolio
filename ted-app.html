TED App

Dual subtitles doubled viewing time for non-English speakers

I led international UX research across 13 languages to understand why TED's global users were dropping off. The insight: non-native English speakers were pausing constantly to look up words, interrupting their viewing experience. I designed a dual-subtitle feature that displays both English and the user's native language simultaneouslyâ€”a simple solution that doubled time spent in the app for this audience.

The challenge

TED's app had strong adoption internationally, but engagement lagged. With more than 75% of users outside the US and roughly half being non-native English speakers, we suspected language barriers were the culprit.

What I did

I conducted remote interviews with 30+ users across 13 countries, focusing on language-learners and non-native speakers. The consistent finding: users valued hearing the original English audio and seeing the English subtitles for learning purposes, but also preferred having subtitles in their native language to follow along. Existing solutions forced a choiceâ€”English OR translated subtitlesâ€”which satisfied neither goal.

The solution

Dual subtitles: Native language on top, English below. I validated the prototype with users in China, Japan, and Korea before shipping.
